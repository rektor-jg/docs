GamerHash is a Polish company founded in 2017. The product is a global, distributed compute platform powered by **idle GPUs in gamers' homes** — and consumed by AI applications that need inference at scale.

## The short version

The platform has three sides:

## What the platform actually does

The **[GamerHash AI App](https://gamerhash.com/en/app-download)** is a free Windows desktop app. When you run it, you can:

- **Use private AI tools locally** — chat, image generation, video generation, multimodal Q&A, all running on your own GPU. No cloud, no logs, no usage caps.
- **Share idle GPU power** — opt in to the earning module, and the app dispatches AI inference jobs to your machine when it's not in use. You get paid in **GUSD** (1 GUSD ≈ 1 USD).
- **Earn GHXP** — engagement points that act as a multiplier on your GPU-sharing rewards. The longer you run the app and hold GHX, the higher your rank.

For developers, the same compute is exposed via **[deAPI](https://deapi.ai)** — an OpenAI-compatible REST API plus an official n8n node, so any application or workflow can route AI workloads to the network without custom infrastructure.

## Why this exists

The demand for AI compute is exploding, while a vast pool of consumer GPUs sits idle most of the day. GamerHash bridges those two: gamers monetize hardware that would otherwise be wasted, and AI builders get compute at a fraction of cloud cost.

The same infrastructure that once powered crypto mining was repointed at AI in 2023 — and PoW mining was wound down on **31 January 2026**. Today the platform is fully focused on AI compute.

## Mission

Turn every home PC into a node in the world's largest distributed compute network — and make participation as simple as installing one app.

## Vision

A future in which AI is not gated by hyperscaler GPU clusters but powered by hundreds of millions of consumer machines around the world, with the value flowing back to the people who own them.
