## 2017 — The Mining App

Two co-founders launched the **GamerHash Miner App** with a simple goal: let anyone earn from idle PC time. Within months, 150,000 users had joined; by mid-2018 the community had doubled. The app abstracted everything users used to hate about mining — wallet management, algorithm selection, payout thresholds — into one click.

## 2019 – 2020 — GamerCoin (GHX)

The native token launched on Ethereum, with the **TGE on 31 December 2020**. The whitepaper was registered with the **Malta Financial Services Authority (MFSA)** — an early commitment to transparency. By April 2021 GHX was listed on Bithumb (then world's third-largest exchange), and bridged to BNB Chain in 2022.

## 2023 — GamerHash AI is born

With open-source AI maturing fast, the team realised the same idle-GPU infrastructure could power something more valuable than PoW hashes. The first version of the **GamerHash AI App** rolled out internally; in **March 2024** the public release added the AI Image Generator module.

## 2024 — Scaling the AI App

A year of breadth: Llama 3.1, Qwen 2.5, Gemma 2, Phi-4, DeepSeek, Stable Diffusion, FLUX. AI Image Booster prompts, AstroGPT upgrades, the GHXP Boost mechanic for GHX holders, and a full redesign of [gamerhash.com](https://gamerhash.com).

## 2025 — Multi-chain and the developer layer

GHX bridged to **Solana** through Raydium and Jupiter; staking and liquidity mining went live at [staking.gamercoin.com](https://staking.gamercoin.com). The AI App added image-to-video and text-to-video. **deAPI** opened to the public — first as a beta API, then with a full playground and an official **n8n** node — turning the GamerHash GPU network into a developer platform.

## 2026 — Pure AI

Crypto mining was wound down on **31 January 2026**. The platform now runs exclusively on AI compute — multimodal models, custom LoRA uploads, Text-to-Speech and Voice Transcription on the way. GamerHash AI App reached 800,000+ users, and the DePIN is the engine.

## What stayed the same

Through every pivot, one principle has held: **the user owns the hardware, and the user gets paid for using it**. Whether the workload was BTC, then GHX, now AI inference, the contract with the contributor never changed.

> _We are here for the gamers. We are the game changer. We are the GamerHash._
