## How it flows back

```
deAPI / partner revenue
   │
   ├── Contributors  →  paid in GUSD per workload completed
   │                    GHXP rank multiplier scales the share
   │
   ├── Platform     →  ops, infra, R&D, support
   │
   └── GHX buyback  →  open-market buys + burn, distributed yield
```

A portion of revenue is used to **buy GHX from the open market and burn it**, plus to fund staking and liquidity-mining yield (up to **25% APR**) on [staking.gamercoin.com](https://staking.gamercoin.com). This ties the token's economics to real platform activity rather than emission schedules alone.

See the [GHX tokenomics docs](https://docs.gamercoin.com/tokenomics/overview) for the full token-side picture: supply, distribution, burn, staking.

## What contributors get

When you run the GamerHash AI App with the earning module enabled:

- **GUSD per workload** — pegged 1:1 to the US dollar, credited automatically as jobs complete.
- **GHXP** — engagement points awarded every minute the app runs. Higher GHXP = higher reward share when revenue is distributed.
- **GHX boost** — holding GHX in MetaMask multiplies your GHXP accrual rate, so token holders earn faster.

You decide what to do with GUSD: convert to **GHX** (HODL or stake for APR), withdraw as **USDC** to Binance, or spend in the **GamerHash Store**.

## What developers pay

Pricing is per-request, by model class, on [deAPI](https://deapi.ai). The same OpenAI-compatible endpoint that powers `gpt-oss-20b` chat will also serve image generation, video, embeddings, and transcription. There's a free tier for evaluation; paid tiers scale via Stripe or crypto payments.

## Why this works

The model is unusual in that **revenue and rewards are denominated in dollars (or GUSD), not emissions**. Mining rewards from a fixed pool fall when more people join; here, the more compute the network sells, the larger the pool that's distributed. As long as deAPI volume grows, contributor earnings grow with it.
